MB-79N
MB 79N is an economical window and door system that meets improved thermal and acoustic insulation regulations

It is the successor of the acclaimed and widely used in the construction industry MB-70 system. Due to its high strength and durability, it creates rich constructional possibilities.

It is used to make a wide range of joinery, including fixed windows, casements, tilt and tilt and slide windows, single- and double-leaf external doors and shop window solutions with doors.

The system comes in several variants
  • the economical MB-79N E, with a single-component central gasket in the windows
  • the MB-79N ST version with a two-component central gasket
  • the MB-79N SI variant with the best thermal insulation and profiles equipped with insulating inserts and a two-component central gasket.
  1. Frame depth 70 mm
  2. Leaf depth 79 mm
  3. Glazing thickness 1,5 - 63 mm
  4. Frame 50,5mm
  5. Leaf from 33,5mm
  6. Max. door leaf dimensions (HxL) H to 2700mm, L to 1700mm
  7. Max. sash weight 180kg

Catalysts for Net Zero Energy Buildings Transformation
Net Zero Energy Buildings (NZEBs) produce as much energy as they consume annually, combining energy efficiency and renewables. NZEBs cut costs, enhance comfort, reduce environmental impact, and ensure energy security. They consist of two components: energy production (utilizing renewables) and energy efficiency (design, materials, and equipment). Windows play a crucial role in NZEBs, aiding ventilation, passive heating/cooling, and insulation. They reduce the need for mechanical systems, optimize temperature, and minimize heat transfer.
Department of Energy guidelines provide insights into NZEB principles for various building types, promoting energy-efficient design and renewables. Vistaza supports sustainability, offering expertise on integrating high-performance windows into NZEBs. Despite budget concerns, investing in these windows enhances energy efficiency, natural lighting, and overall project sustainability. Enhancing Aesthetics and Longevity: The Superiority of uPVC Window Foil Over Paint

Colored uPVC windows offer a versatile alternative to traditional wood, combining aesthetics with low maintenance and energy efficiency. Foil-wrapped uPVC windows, using a heat bonding process, closely mimic the texture and color of wood, providing a durable and authentic appearance. Advantages include authentic wood grain, durability (30+ years lifespan), low maintenance, and excellent energy efficiency.

Foil-wrapped uPVC windows also provide design versatility, allowing customization to match individual preferences. While repairing uPVC foil is possible, painting uPVC windows is generally not recommended due to potential drawbacks like reduced lifespan, maintenance issues, and warranty concerns.

In conclusion, foil-wrapped uPVC windows offer a superior solution for coloring uPVC frames, providing durability, low maintenance, and energy efficiency. While there may be instances where paint is preferred, choosing foil-wrapped uPVC windows is generally recommended for a long-lasting and aesthetically pleasing result.

Green Horizons: PHI and PHIUS Windows Paving the Way for Eco-Conscious Designs
Within the domain of sustainable architecture, we delve into the principles and certifications of Passive House, as established by the esteemed Passive House Institute (PHI) and Passive House Institute US (PHIUS). Understanding Passive House and Certification:
Passive Houses, pioneers in energy-efficient havens prioritizing thermal comfort, undergo rigorous certification by either PHI or PHIUS. This certification attests to their commitment to energy conservation and sustainable living environments.

PHI vs. PHIUS

PHI and PHIUS, though sharing the goal of promoting energy-efficient construction, diverge in their approaches and standards due to varied climate conditions. PHI's European Standard caters to the international market, while PHIUS tailors its American Standard to North American climate needs.

Key Similarities and Differences

While both certifications use similar metrics, variations exist in measurement units and criteria. Airtightness, heating and cooling requirements, and energy calculations differ, demanding architects' awareness to ensure compliance with specific standards.

Benefits of PHI and PHIUS-Certified Windows

PHI and PHIUS-certified windows offer a plethora of advantages, contributing to a greener environment and reducing energy consumption. These windows play a pivotal role in achieving Passive House certification, enhancing overall energy efficiency, and ensuring optimal thermal comfort. SKYGEN Windows emerged as a key player, offering high-quality, energy-efficient windows aligned with PHIUS certification standards. With a commitment to sustainability and innovation, SKYGEN provides architects with a diverse range of windows designed to optimize energy performance and enhance comfort. Architects and builders find a clear roadmap to energy-efficient and sustainable building design through PHI and PHIUS certifications. This guide unravels the nuances, empowering architects to make informed decisions. With support from companies like SKYGEN, providing high-quality, certified windows, architects can seize the window of opportunity to elevate their Passive House projects.
